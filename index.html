I am a Bioinformatics PhD student at the Institute for Molecular Bioscience at the University of Queensland. I work under the supervision of Dr. Ryan Taft, Associate Professor Ernst Wolvetang, Dr. Cas Simons and Professor John Mattick.

My research focuses on using high-throughput sequencing to understand changes in the transcriptome that occur during neuronal functioning in normal cells and in disease; and on induced pluripotent stem cells as models of the human nervous system. I am also passionate about teaching, especially programming and bioinformatics to biologists.

I hold a Specialist Degree in Biochemistry with a Major in Molecular Biology from Lomonosov Moscow State University.

I blog on next generation sequencing data analysis and maintain a personal wiki to collect relevant code, techniques and recommendations from the web and genomics software mailing lists. I am also an active tweeter.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, hiking/bushwalking and learning about the history and culture of the places I am fortunate enough to live in or travel to.
